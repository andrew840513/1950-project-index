Lab

Student Pages

All your homework must reside within the student pages provided for this course. This is so the instructor can track your progress and examine any code that may be server-processed.

You can use any FTP program you are comfortable with. There is most likely one installed in the labs (it changes from term to term) but you can download and install any one you want.

Filezilla is a great FTP client that works on Mac/PC/Linux if you are looking for one to install on your laptop or at home.

If you aren't knowledgeable with any FTP programs yet, just let me know!

Account details

An account is created for each student that gives them 200MB of space, and a URL they can navigate to.

How to find your URL

Your student page will always be at:

http://students.thenet.ca/{student-account}

The {student-account} part is almost always your first initial and last name. For instance, John Smith would end up as jsmith

How to FTP to your account

In order to log into your FTP account, you will need:

Hostname: thenet.ca
							Username: {student-account}@thenet.ca
							Password: {whatever you create}

Please take note that your username must end in @thenet.ca for it to work.

Assignment

Homework will ALWAYS be due at 12:00pm Noon the day before the next class. For those who are challenged by calendars, this means that you must have your homework uploaded to your Student Page by Noon on Mondays.

If you do not have your homework up in time, it will be marked as incomplete and you will receive no feedback. However, you are encouraged to complete your homework anyway, as you will receive a mark at the end of the term for the entirety of your assignments.
